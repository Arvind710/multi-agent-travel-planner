# Problem Statement: AI-Powered Multi-Agent Travel Planner for India

**Working name:** (TBD) — referred to as "the Planner" below
**Document purpose:** Complete product specification / problem statement for building a production-ready web application, developed end-to-end with AI (Antigravity).
**Version:** 1.0 — July 2026

---

## 1. Problem Statement

Planning a trip in India is disproportionately hard compared to most destinations. Existing tools (MakeMyTrip, Google Travel, generic ChatGPT itineraries, travel blogs) fail travellers in specific ways:

1. **Generic itineraries.** "Golden Triangle in 5 days" lists that ignore who the traveller is, what they love, what they hate, and what pace they can sustain.
2. **India-blind planning.** Tools ignore the monsoon calendar, monument closure days, rail booking windows and waitlist risk, permit requirements for restricted regions, altitude acclimatization, festival surges, realistic Himalayan road speeds, and air quality seasons.
3. **No reasoning.** Users get a list of places with no explanation of *why* this hotel, *why* this sequence, *why* these dates. They can't trust or intelligently modify the plan.
4. **Form fatigue.** Traditional planners front-load 30+ form fields. Users abandon them. Conversely, pure-chat planners never gather enough signal to be personal.
5. **Fragile plans.** No plan Bs. One fog-cancelled Delhi flight or waitlisted train collapses the whole itinerary, and the tool offers no recovery path.
6. **Taste is absent.** No existing tool distinguishes a traveller who wants the heritage haveli, the weaver's workshop and the correct sunrise ghat from one who wants a mall, a chain hotel and a checklist selfie at the Taj.

**The product:** A web application where a traveller describes their trip in plain natural language, is asked a small number of intelligent clarifying questions, optionally enriches their profile through structured inputs, and receives a deeply personalised, fully reasoned, logistics-complete, link-rich itinerary for travel in India — generated by a cooperating system of specialised AI agents, and refinable through conversation.

**Positioning in one line:** *A brilliant, obsessive, India-expert friend who plans your trip, shows their working, and fixes it when things change.*

## 2. Target Users & Personas

| Persona | Description | Key needs |
|---|---|---|
| **First-time foreign visitor** | International tourist, 1–3 weeks, anxious about logistics/safety/food | Hand-holding, visa/permit guidance, comfort floors, safety notes, realistic pacing |
| **Domestic explorer** | Indian metro resident planning long weekends / annual leave trips | Offbeat suggestions, budget optimisation, train/flight realism, festival awareness |
| **NRI returning with family** | Mixed-age group, mixing family obligations with tourism | Multi-city routing, elderly/kid constraints, comfort, flexible anchors (wedding dates etc.) |
| **Luxury/taste-driven traveller** | High budget, low tolerance for mediocrity | Curation quality, heritage properties, food-as-destination, crowd avoidance, exclusivity |

| **Special-interest traveller** | Wildlife, trekking, textiles, spirituality, photography, food | Deep domain routing (park zones, trek seasons, craft clusters, ashram protocols) |
| **Solo female traveller** | Any budget | Safety-scored routing, accommodation vetting, arrival-time logic, transport choices |
| **Business + leisure ("bleisure")** | Fixed work anchor days, free evenings/weekends | Plans around fixed commitments, IST work hours, wifi-reliable stays |

## 3. Core User Journey

```
1. LANDING      → User types/speaks a free-form trip description (or picks an inspiration template)
2. UNDERSTAND   → System parses the prompt, builds a partial Traveller Profile, shows what it understood
3. CLARIFY      → System asks 3–8 targeted clarifying questions (chat-style, quick-tap answers)
4. ENRICH (opt) → User may open the "Add detail" panel: structured sliders/pickers for every variable
5. GENERATE     → Multi-agent pipeline produces the plan (streamed progress: "checking rail routes…",
                  "validating monsoon risk…", "scoring 42 stays against your taste profile…")
6. PRESENT      → Rich itinerary document: day-by-day plan, reasoning, links, maps, budget, plan Bs
7. REFINE       → User edits via chat ("less temples, more food", "swap Jaipur for Bundi") or inline controls
8. FINALISE     → Export (PDF/print/calendar), share with co-travellers, booking handoff links
9. (POST-MVP) LIVE MODE → During the trip: day cards, alerts, live re-planning on disruption
```

## 4. Input Layer

### 4.1 Natural Language First (primary input)

The entry point is a single prompt box. Examples of real inputs the system must handle:

- *"2 weeks in India in December with my wife, love food and old architecture, hate crowds, mid-range budget, flying into Delhi"*
- *"solo female, first time in India, 10 days, want spirituality and mountains but I'm scared of getting sick"*
- *"family trip, parents are 65+, kids 8 and 11, somewhere not too hot in May, from Bangalore, max 60k per person"*
- *"honeymoon, money no object, want it to feel like a movie"*
- *"I have a wedding in Udaipur Nov 14–16, want to build a 9-day trip around it"*

**Requirements:**

- **Entity & preference extraction.** Parse dates (absolute, relative, fuzzy — "sometime in winter"), duration, origin, party composition, budget (absolute, per-person, qualitative), interests, anti-preferences, constraints, fixed anchors (weddings, conferences), emotional tone ("scared of getting sick" → comfort floor + health guidance flags).
- **Confidence tagging.** Every extracted field carries a confidence score. Low-confidence and missing high-impact fields drive the clarifying-question engine.
- **Understanding echo.** Before asking anything, show the user a compact "Here's what I understood" card (editable chips: `Dec 2026 · 14 days · Couple · Mid-range · Loves: food, architecture · Avoids: crowds · Entry: Delhi`). Users correct misreads by tapping, not re-typing.
- **Multilingual input.** Accept Hindi, Hinglish, and major Indian languages plus English at minimum. Respond in the user's language.
- **Voice input** on mobile web.
- **Inspiration mode.** For users who don't know what they want: "Surprise me — I have 6 days and ₹40k" is a valid prompt. The system proposes 3 contrasting trip concepts first (e.g., *Malabar coast slow trip* vs *Rajasthan heritage circuit* vs *Himalayan village stay*), and the user picks one to develop.

### 4.2 Clarifying Question Engine

The system asks follow-up questions **based on what the user said and what's missing** — never a fixed questionnaire.

**Design rules:**

1. **Budget of 3–8 questions maximum** per session before first plan generation. Rank candidate questions by *information value × plan impact* and ask only the top ones.
2. **Question selection is context-dependent.** Examples:
   - User said "December, mountains" → ask about snow/cold tolerance and altitude comfort (Ladakh roads closed; Himachal viable).
   - User said "family, parents 65+" → ask about walking tolerance, dietary needs, hotel comfort floor.
   - User said "love food" → ask ONE sharp taste question: "Street food from the famous stall, or the region's best-reviewed restaurants — where do you lean?"
   - User gave dates during Diwali → ask whether they want to lean into the festival or avoid the chaos/pricing.
   - Foreign traveller → ask nationality (visa/permit logic), prior India experience.
3. **Quick-tap answers.** Every question ships with 3–5 tappable options + free text. Answering should take seconds.
4. **Every question shows its "because".** *"Asking because Ladakh's road routes close by mid-October — this decides whether mountains are on the table."* This builds trust and teaches the user.
5. **Skippable.** "Just plan it" is always available; the system proceeds with sensible defaults and marks assumptions explicitly in the output.
6. **Post-draft questions are cheaper.** People reveal taste better by reacting than by answering forms. The system deliberately defers low-impact questions and instead learns from the user's edits to draft 1.

### 4.3 Optional Structured Detail Panel ("Add detail")

An expandable panel (never mandatory, collapsed by default) where power users can specify every variable directly. Pre-filled with whatever the NL parser extracted. Organised in four tabs:

**Tab A — Trip fundamentals**
| Field | Input type | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Travel dates | Date range + flexibility slider (±0–7 days) | Flexibility unlocks fare/weather optimisation |
| Duration | Nights count (if dates fuzzy) | |
| Origin city / country | Autocomplete | Drives arrival logistics & visa logic |
| Entry / exit airports | Autocomplete, "let the planner decide" default | |
| Traveller nationality & visa status | Select | e-Visa eligibility, permit rules |
| Party composition | Structured: adults, children (ages), seniors (ages) | |
| Mobility / accessibility needs | Multi-select + free text | Wheelchair, limited stairs, walking distance caps |
| Total budget | Amount + currency + what it includes (flights? shopping?) | Also accepts qualitative: shoestring / value / comfort / luxury / uncapped |
| Budget split preference | Sliders: stays vs food vs experiences vs transport | "Splurge on hotels, save on transport" |
| Fixed anchors | Date + place + event entries | Weddings, meetings, reunions the plan must respect |

**Tab B — Taste profile**
| Field | Input type | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Pace | Slider: "2 places, deeply" ↔ "see everything" | Single highest-impact taste variable |
| Interest weights | Weighted tags (0–5 each): architecture & heritage, food & drink, wildlife & nature, trekking & adventure, spirituality & wellness, arts/textiles/crafts, music & performance, history, beaches, nightlife, photography, shopping, offbeat/rural life | Weights, not checkboxes |
| Anti-preferences | Free multi-select: crowds, long drives, early mornings, guided tours, temples, forts, "touristy" places, spicy food, street food, overnight travel | Explicitly asked — highest-signal field |
| Crowd tolerance | Slider: iconic-even-if-mobbed ↔ offbeat-only | |
| Accommodation taste | Multi-select: heritage haveli/palace, boutique, luxury chain, business hotel, homestay, eco-lodge, hostel, camps/glamping | Taste, not star-rating |
| Food adventurousness | Slider + flags: veg / vegan / Jain / halal / kosher; spice tolerance; street food yes/no; food-as-destination interest | |
| Prior India experience | None / some / extensive + places already visited | First-timers vs returnees get different plans |
| Reference trips | Free text: "a trip you loved and why; one you hated and why" | Richest single taste signal |
| Trip vibe | Free text / tags: romantic, restorative, adventurous, cinematic, social, solitary | |

**Tab C — Comfort & practical constraints**
| Field | Input type |
|---|---|
| Transport comfort floor | Multi-select: flights only, AC trains OK, non-AC OK, overnight trains OK, overnight buses OK, private car preferred, self-drive, happy on local transport |
| Max single-day travel time | Slider (hours) |
| Altitude sensitivity | Yes/no/unknown (triggers acclimatization design & medical notes) |
| Health & dietary constraints | Free text + structured flags |
| Heat / cold tolerance | Sliders |
| Safety posture | Standard / heightened (drives area, arrival-time, transport choices) |
| Work constraints | Needs reliable wifi / call hours to protect |
| Language comfort | English only / Hindi / regional languages |
| Travel insurance status | Have / need guidance |

**Tab D — Output preferences**
| Field | Input type |
|---|---|
| Plan detail level | Slider: "just the skeleton" ↔ "hour-by-hour with alternatives" |
| Reasoning verbosity | Show all reasoning / key decisions only |
| Currency & units | Select |
| Output language | Select |

**Interaction rule:** anything answered in chat auto-fills this panel; anything set in the panel is respected by chat. One profile, two views.

```json
  "trip": {
    "dates": {"start": "2026-12-05", "end": "2026-12-19", "flexibility_days": 3, "confidence": 0.9},
    "origin": {"city": "London", "country": "GB"},
    "entry_exit": {"entry": "DEL", "exit": "auto", "confidence": 0.7},
    "anchors": [{"place": "Udaipur", "dates": ["2026-12-14","2026-12-16"], "event": "wedding", "hard": true}]
  },
  "party": {"adults": 2, "children": [], "seniors": [], "solo_female": false},
  "budget": {"total": 350000, "currency": "INR", "per_person": false, "includes_flights": false,
             "tier": "upper-mid", "split_bias": {"stays": 0.4, "food": 0.25, "experiences": 0.25, "transport": 0.1}},
  "taste": {
    "pace": 0.3,

    "anti": ["crowds", "early_mornings", "guided_tours"],
    "crowd_tolerance": 0.2,
    "stay_styles": ["heritage", "boutique"],
    "food": {"diet": "none", "spice": 0.7, "street_food": true, "food_as_destination": true},
    "experience_level": "first_time",
    "references": {"loved": "Kyoto — old streets, small restaurants", "hated": "Dubai — malls and traffic"}
  },
  "constraints": {
    "transport_floor": ["flights", "ac_trains", "private_car"],
    "max_daily_travel_hours": 5,
    "altitude_ok": "unknown",
    "health": [], "diet_flags": [],
    "visa": {"nationality": "GB", "evisa_eligible": true, "status": "not_applied"}
  },

```

**Provenance matters:** every field records *where it came from* (parsed / asked / form / inferred from an edit), so the system can distinguish "user said" from "we assumed" — and the output can honestly label assumptions.

## 5. Planning Engine: Multi-Agent Architecture

### 5.1 Agent roster

| Agent | Role | Key responsibilities |
|---|---|---|
| **Profiler** | Owns the Traveller Profile | NL extraction, clarifying-question selection, inferring taste from edits, profile versioning |
| **Concept Architect** | Trip-level shape | Chooses region(s), theme, and route skeleton; generates 2–3 contrasting concepts when the brief is open-ended |
| **Constraint Guardian** | Hard filters — runs FIRST and LAST | Seasonality/monsoon windows, permits (ILP for Arunachal/Nagaland/Mizoram/Sikkim border areas, PAP for parts of Ladakh & Andamans), park closures (most wildlife parks closed Jul–Sep), monument closure days (Taj: Fridays), festival calendars & surge windows, visa rules, altitude acclimatization rules, landslide/road-closure seasons, AQI seasons (NCR in Nov), regional advisories |
| **Route Optimizer** | Sequencing & transit | Geographic clustering, travel-time realism (Himachal ≠ expressway speeds), rail booking-window & waitlist-probability logic, flight connectivity of tier-2 airports, energy-curve pacing, buffer-day placement, arrival-time safety logic |
| **Stay Curator** | Accommodation | Scores properties against taste profile (heritage/boutique/homestay…), location logic (walkability to the old city vs highway hotels), verified links, price bands, 2 alternates per night |
| **Experience Curator** | Things to do, with taste | The "high taste" agent: the specific thali house, the weaver's workshop in the craft cluster, the correct fort gate, sunrise/sunset timing, crowd-avoidance timing, one "earned" offbeat gem per leg, bookable-experience links |
| **Food Curator** | Eating as a first-class dimension | Regional cuisine mapping, dish-level recommendations ("what to order"), hygiene-confidence tiers for street food, dietary-flag filtering |
| **Logistics Agent** | Every A→B | Trains (numbers, classes, timings, booking windows, tatkal fallback), flights, private cars with realistic durations, airport transfers, intra-city transport per city (metro/auto/Uber availability), booking links for each leg |
| **Budget Reconciler** | Money | Line-item costing, tier consistency (no ₹2k hotel next to a ₹30k one without reason), fee/tax/tip realism, foreign-exchange notes, runs after every revision |
| **Risk & Resilience Agent** | Plan Bs | Disruption probability per leg (fog at DEL in Dec–Jan, monsoon landslides, waitlist risk), a fallback for every fragile leg, travel insurance pointers, emergency info per city |
| **Critic** | Adversarial review — the quality gate | Attacks the draft before the user sees it: "Day 4 is 9 hours of driving for one fort", "You promised low-crowd but routed Amber Fort at 11am on a Saturday", "This pace violates the stated energy preference", "Two heavy Mughal-architecture days back-to-back for a food-first traveller". Draft loops until the Critic passes it or max iterations reached |
| **Narrator** | Output assembly | Converts the validated plan graph into the user-facing document: prose, reasoning callouts, links, maps, exports — in the user's language and requested detail level |

### 5.2 Orchestration flow

```
Profiler ─► Concept Architect ─► Constraint Guardian (filter) ─► Route Optimizer
                                                                     │
              ┌──────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
              ▼                    ▼                  ▼               ▼
        Stay Curator      Experience Curator    Food Curator    Logistics Agent
              └──────────────────────┬───────────────────────────────┘
                                     ▼
                            Budget Reconciler
                                     ▼
                          Risk & Resilience Agent
                                     ▼
                                  CRITIC ──fail──► targeted revision loop (max N iters)
                                     │pass
                                     ▼
                          Constraint Guardian (final re-validation)
                                     ▼
                                 Narrator ─► user
```

**Engineering requirements:**

- **Shared plan graph.** Agents read/write a typed itinerary graph (days → segments → bookable items), not free text. Every node carries `reasoning`, `confidence`, `sources[]`, `alternatives[]`, `price`, `links[]`.
- **Grounding, not hallucination.** Any factual claim (hotel exists, train runs on Tuesdays, park is open) must be backed by a tool call to a live source or a versioned knowledge base entry — never model memory alone. Unverifiable items are labelled "verify before booking".
- **Streaming progress.** Users see which agent is doing what in real time. Perceived latency is a feature surface.
- **Revision is targeted.** "Swap Jaipur for Bundi" re-runs only affected agents on affected nodes, not the whole pipeline.
- **Determinism where possible.** Constraint checks (dates vs closure calendars, permit rules) are code, not LLM judgment.

### 5.3 India knowledge base (first-class asset)

A maintained, versioned dataset the agents query — this is the moat:

- Monsoon & climate calendar by region/month (incl. inversions: Kerala peak-wet Jun–Aug vs Rajasthan; Ladakh's May–Sep window; Tamil Nadu's NE monsoon Oct–Dec)
- Festival calendar with pricing/crowd impact radius (Diwali, Holi, Pushkar Mela, Durga Puja, Onam, Hornbill, Rann Utsav…)
- Permit matrix: region × nationality × permit type × lead time × application channel
- Monument DB: closure days, timings, entry fees (domestic/foreign), dress codes, photography rules, non-Hindu entry restrictions, best-time-of-day
- Wildlife park DB: safari zones, booking windows, closure seasons, sighting realism
- Rail knowledge: booking window (60 days), tatkal mechanics, class guide (1A/2A/3A/CC/SL), waitlist-probability heuristics, scenic routes worth taking for their own sake
- Road realism table: effective km/h by terrain (plains expressway ~70, Himalayan ~25–35), landslide seasons
- Altitude protocol: acclimatization day rules for Leh (3,500m), Spiti, Sikkim high passes
- AQI seasonal patterns, heat-wave calendars
- Safety notes layer: per-city arrival-time guidance, area awareness, solo-female-specific notes, current advisories
- Regional food atlas: cuisine → signature dishes → where locals actually eat
- Craft/textile cluster map (Kutch, Chettinad, Varanasi weaves, Channapatna, Moradabad…)

## 6. Output Specification

The output is a **document + interactive surface**, not a chat message. Every plan contains:

### 6.1 Trip Overview
- Title & one-paragraph narrative of the trip's logic ("This trip trades breadth for depth: two bases, six days each…")
- Route map (interactive; every stop pinned)
- Trip stats: cities, nights per stop, total transit hours, transit-to-experience ratio, walking level, budget total vs stated budget
- **"Why this trip" panel** — the 5–8 major decisions and their reasoning, e.g.:
  - *"Udaipur over Jaipur: you said crowds are a dealbreaker; Jaipur in late December runs at peak domestic-tourism density. Udaipur delivers the same Rajput architecture register at ~40% of the crowd load."*
  - *"December dates locked to Dec 5–19 within your ±3-day flexibility: shifts you clear of the Christmas fare spike (~22% on DEL-UDR sector) and puts you in Udaipur for the quiet week."*
- **Assumptions ledger** — every default the system chose because the user didn't specify, each one tappable to change: *"Assumed: no overnight trains (you didn't say — tap to allow and save ~₹14,000)."*
- 2–3 **discarded alternatives** with honest reasons ("We considered adding Jodhpur — cut because it forces two 5-hour drives that violate your pace preference").

### 6.2 Day-by-Day Plan
For every day:
- **Day header:** date, city, weather normals for that date, sunrise/sunset, day's theme, energy rating (light/moderate/full)
- **Timeline blocks** (morning/afternoon/evening or hour-by-hour per user's detail preference). Each block:
  - What, where (map-linked), duration, cost
  - **Why** — one crisp reasoning line tied to the user's profile ("You rated crafts 3/5 — this block is the block-printing workshop in Bagru, not the tourist showroom version")
  - **Insider layer:** the correct entrance, what to order, when the crowds arrive, what to skip inside a large site, dress code, photography rules
  - Booking/info links where applicable
  - **Swap options:** 1–2 alternatives per major block, one-tap swappable
- **Meals:** named venue per meal slot with dish-level guidance and a fallback option; dietary flags respected
- **Buffer & rest honesty:** free time explicitly scheduled and defended ("Nothing planned after 4pm — day 6 is your highest-fatigue day")

### 6.3 Stays
Per stop: recommended property + 2 alternates (one cheaper, one splurge). Each with:
- Direct link (property site + at least one OTA), neighbourhood-fit reasoning, price/night estimate & cancellation-policy note, distance-to-plan analysis ("8 min walk to the old-city gate where days 3–4 happen"), taste-match note ("150-year-old restored haveli — matches your heritage preference; note: stairs, no lift" if mobility flags exist)

### 6.4 Logistics Dossier
- **Every inter-city leg:** mode, operator, train number/flight options, duration (realistic), class recommendation & why, price estimate, **booking link + when to book** ("IRCTC opens this train Oct 6 — book that day; current waitlist pattern for 2A clears ~80%")
- Arrival/departure playbooks: airport→hotel options with prices, prepaid-taxi vs Uber guidance, SIM/eSIM purchase, ATM/cash notes
- Intra-city transport card per city (metro? Uber reliability? auto fare norms?)
- Visa/permit checklist with application links, fees, lead times, document lists — sequenced into a pre-trip timeline

### 6.5 Budget Sheet
- Line-item table by day and by category; totals vs stated budget; per-person split
- Confidence bands (booked-price vs estimate), seasonal-surge warnings
- "Where your money goes" summary and 2–3 costed trade-offs ("Drop to 3A on the overnight train: –₹9,400; upgrade Udaipur stay to the palace: +₹38,000")

### 6.6 Risk & Plan-B Annex
- Fragile-leg register: each risky leg, its failure probability & cause, and a concrete pre-planned fallback
- Health notes personalised to the profile (altitude protocol days if relevant, food-safety practices, recommended kit)
- Emergency card per city: nearest reputable hospital, police (100/112), tourist helpline (1363), embassy if foreign
- Weather-disruption playbook for the season

### 6.7 Pre-Trip Timeline & Packing
- Countdown checklist auto-generated from the plan: T-60 book trains, T-45 apply e-Visa, T-30 book safaris, T-7 download offline maps…
- Packing list derived from region × season × activities × party (altitude meds flag, modest-dress items for religious sites, monsoon gear)

### 6.8 Formats & Sharing
- Interactive web view (canonical), beautiful **PDF export**, **calendar export** (.ics with locations), print-friendly one-page-per-day version, offline-readable version
- **Share link** for co-travellers (view or comment); WhatsApp-friendly summary format (critical for India)

## 7. Refinement & Conversation Loop

- **Chat-based editing is the primary revision tool:** "make day 5 lighter", "we now have one extra day", "my mother is joining, she can't do stairs" — each triggers targeted re-planning with a **diff view** ("Here's what changed and why").
- **Inline controls:** drag to reorder days, tap to swap any block/stay/leg with its listed alternatives, lock elements ("keep this hotel no matter what").
- **Taste learning:** every edit updates the Traveller Profile (with provenance `inferred_from_edit`) and is confirmed lightly: "Noted — fewer forts, more markets. Applied to the rest of the trip too."
- **Version history:** every generation is saved; users can compare and roll back.
- **Regenerate scopes:** whole trip / one city / one day / one block.

## 8. Additional Features Users Will Want (build-in or fast-follow)

**Accounts & persistence**
- Save multiple trips, multiple traveller profiles ("me solo" vs "family mode"), returning-user memory ("Last time you loved the homestay — weight those higher?")

**Collaboration**
- Invite co-travellers; they can react/vote on blocks; a "group taste merge" that reconciles conflicting preferences and shows the compromise reasoning

**Booking & money**
- Deep links with dates/party pre-filled (IRCTC, airline sites, hotel sites/OTAs, GetYourGuide/Viator-style experience platforms)
- Price-watch: track a flight/hotel and notify when to book (email/WhatsApp)
- (Later) affiliate/booking integration as the revenue model

**Trip-time companion (post-MVP, high retention value)**
- "Today" card each morning: plan, weather, bookings, what to wear/carry
- Live re-planning: "our train is 4 hours late" → recovery plan in one message
- Nearby-now suggestions consistent with the taste profile

**Trust & transparency**
- Sources shown on factual claims; "last verified" timestamps on hours/fees
- Clear "verify before booking" labels on anything unconfirmed
- Feedback loop: post-trip "what was great / what missed" → improves both the user's profile and global curation quality

**Accessibility & reach**
- Full mobile-web excellence (majority of Indian traffic), PWA + offline itinerary access
- Hindi + major Indian language output; screen-reader compliant; low-bandwidth mode
- WhatsApp delivery of the itinerary and alerts

**Delight**
- Trip "movie poster" / shareable visual summary
- "Local phrasebook" card per region (10 useful phrases, transliterated)
- Spotify-style trip playlist / reading list matched to destinations (a Kerala trip suggests *The God of Small Things*)
- Post-trip photo-map memory page

## 9. Non-Functional Requirements

| Area | Requirement |
|---|---|
| **Latency** | First streamed content < 5s; full plan < 90s with visible agent progress; targeted revisions < 20s |
| **Grounding** | Zero-tolerance policy for invented hotels/trains/timings; all factual nodes carry a source or a "verify" label |
| **Cost control** | Per-plan LLM cost budget; cache constraint-check results, KB lookups, and stable sub-plans; small models for extraction/classification, large models for curation/critique |
| **Reliability** | Pipeline is resumable; single-agent failure degrades gracefully (plan ships with a labelled gap, not an error page) |
| **Security & privacy** | Profiles contain sensitive data (health, nationality, travel dates = occupancy signal). Encrypt at rest, minimal retention, no sale of data, clear consent for memory features. DPDP Act (India) + GDPR compliance |
| **Safety** | Advisory-aware; never route into currently restricted/unsafe areas; conservative defaults for solo travellers on arrival-time logic |
| **Observability** | Full trace of every agent step per plan (inputs, outputs, tool calls) for debugging and quality audits |
| **Evaluation** | Golden-set of ~100 diverse briefs with expert-rated ideal plans; automated Critic-score regression on every prompt/model change; A/B on question strategies |

## 10. External Data & API Surface (candidates — verify availability & terms at build time)

**Bootstrap rule:** at launch, only free-tier sources are live (maps within free credit, Open-Meteo, public data). Flights/hotels/rail run **deep-link-first**: the plan links users to booking sites with details pre-filled, and prices are labelled estimates. Paid APIs below are the scale-phase upgrade path — see architecture §10.1.

| Need | Candidate sources |
|---|---|
| Flights (search + deep link) | Bootstrap: Google Flights/Skyscanner deep links. Scale: Amadeus / Kiwi APIs |
| Trains | Bootstrap: KB schedule knowledge + IRCTC/ConfirmTkt deep links. Scale: partner availability APIs |
| Hotels | Bootstrap: KB-curated + Google Places + OTA deep links. Scale: Booking.com / Agoda / MMT affiliate APIs |
| Maps, places, routing | Google Maps Platform (Places, Directions with traffic, Distance Matrix); Mapbox alternative |
| Weather | Open-Meteo / IMD data for normals + forecast |
| AQI | CPCB / IQAir |
| Experiences | Viator / GetYourGuide / Headout APIs |

## 11. Suggested Technical Shape (for the Antigravity build)

*Recommendation, not mandate — adjust to what the AI toolchain builds best.*

- **Frontend:** Next.js (React) PWA; server-streamed plan rendering; map via Google Maps JS/Mapbox GL; mobile-first
- **Backend:** TypeScript (Node/Nest or Next API routes) or Python (FastAPI); agent orchestration via a graph framework (LangGraph-style) or hand-rolled state machine — favour explicit, debuggable orchestration over framework magic
- **LLMs:** Router pattern — small/fast model for extraction & classification; frontier model for Concept, Experience Curation, Critic, Narrator. All agents behind one internal interface so models are swappable
- **Datastores:** Postgres (profiles, trips, plan graphs, versions), Redis (cache/session), object storage (exports), vector store for the India KB retrieval layer
- **KB pipeline:** versioned content repo (structured YAML/JSON per entity) + ingestion into the vector/lookup layer; every entry has `last_verified` metadata
- **Auth:** email/OTP + Google; phone-OTP matters for India
- **Infra:** containerised; queue-based plan generation (plans are jobs, not request/response); WebSocket/SSE for streaming progress
- **Payments-ready** (future): Razorpay for India, Stripe international

## 12. Success Metrics

- **Activation:** % of visitors who submit a prompt; % who reach a generated plan
- **Plan quality:** Critic pass rate; user edit-depth (moderate editing = engagement; extreme editing = bad drafts); explicit plan rating
- **Trust:** % of factual spot-checks passed (audited weekly); "verify" label rate trending down
- **Retention:** trips saved, return sessions, share-link usage, companion-mode adoption
- **Efficiency:** cost per generated plan; p95 generation latency
- **North star:** *% of generated plans that users actually travel* (proxy: calendar exports + booking-link clicks + post-trip feedback submitted)

## 13. Phasing

**Build posture (governing constraint):** this is a solo hobbyist project with a near-zero cash budget. Every phase must be buildable and runnable by one person on free tiers (target < $20/month infra + LLM API spend capped per plan). Anything requiring a paid API contract, a business entity, or hired staff is *scale-phase* by definition — the architecture keeps a seam for it, but the shipping product never depends on it. Where this document says "human audit" or "curation", the human is the founder.

**Coverage posture:** the planner covers **all of India from day one**. The pan-India constraint datasets (climate, permits, festivals, rail rules, road realism) are mandatory at launch — they are what make plans safe and feasible anywhere. The *curation* KB is a quality tier on top, not a coverage boundary: destinations with deep KB entries get "curated" plans; everywhere else gets grounded plans from live data (Google Places, official sources) with honest depth labelling. Hand-curation is optional and demand-driven — usage data decides which entities are worth founder verification time, ~30 minutes at a time. Rajasthan and Kerala are the initial *showcase* circuits, not the product's borders.

**Phase 1 — MVP (the magic loop)**
NL input → understanding echo → clarifying questions (3–8) → multi-agent generation → full reasoned output (overview, day-by-day, stays, logistics, budget, plan Bs, links) → chat refinement → PDF/share export. India KB v1 covering the 12 highest-demand circuits (Rajasthan, Kerala, Golden Triangle ± extensions, Himachal, Ladakh, Goa, Varanasi–Lucknow, Northeast intro, Tamil Nadu temple trail, Karnataka, Andamans, Uttarakhand).

**Phase 2 — Depth & trust**
Optional detail panel, accounts & saved profiles, price-watch, collaboration/voting, Hindi output, versioning/diff UI, booking deep-link polish, evaluation harness.

**Phase 3 — Companion & scale**
Live trip mode with re-planning, WhatsApp channel, group taste merge, affiliate monetisation, remaining-regions KB coverage, additional Indian languages.

## 14. Key Risks & Mitigations

| Risk | Mitigation |
|---|---|
| Hallucinated facts destroy trust instantly | Grounding policy (§5.2), verify labels, weekly fact audits, KB `last_verified` discipline |
| Taste layer regresses to generic top-10 lists | Critic explicitly checks personalisation; curation KB is human-seeded; edit-signal learning |
| IRCTC/train data access is hard | Design logistics agent to degrade to schedule-knowledge + booking guidance with deep links; partner APIs when available |
| Generation cost per plan too high | Model routing, caching, targeted revisions instead of full regenerations |
| Users overwhelmed by detail | Detail-level output preference (§4.3 Tab D); progressive disclosure — skeleton first, expand on demand |
| Festival/closure data goes stale | KB entries carry expiry & verification dates; stale entries auto-downgrade to "verify" labels |
| Cold-start users with vague briefs | Inspiration mode with contrasting concepts (§4.1) |

## 15. Definition of "Sophisticated, Personalised, High-Taste" (acceptance criteria)

A generated plan passes only if:

1. Every major decision (region, sequence, each stay, ≥1 block per day) has a stated reason **traceable to the user's profile**.
2. Zero hard-constraint violations (closures, permits, seasons, booking windows, monument days).
3. Transit-to-experience ratio within the user's pace preference; no day violates the max-travel-hours constraint.
4. At least one non-obvious, profile-matched "earned" recommendation per leg that a top-10 list would not surface.
5. Anti-preferences are never violated (a "hates crowds" user is never routed into a peak-crowd slot without an explicit flagged trade-off).
6. Every bookable item has a working link or an honest "book via X, opens on date Y" instruction.
7. A plan B exists for every leg flagged fragile.
8. Budget total within ±10% of stated budget, or the deviation is explicitly justified.
9. All assumptions are labelled and editable.
10. The Critic agent signs off, and final constraint re-validation passes.
